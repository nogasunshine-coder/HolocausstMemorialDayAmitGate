📖 משימת פתיחה / Warm-Up
Read the conversation and highlight the people and places they talk about.
👤 Person 📍 Place לחצו על מילה לבחירת קטגוריה

Safta Sara: Oh look, it's my friend Rachel from Amsterdam in Holland. That's the same place where Anne Frank lived.


Itamar: Anne Frank? Who's that?


Safta Sara: Well, Anne was a Jewish girl, like us. During the Holocaust — a time when the Nazis wanted to "hurt" Jewish people — she and her family hid in a secret attic behind a bookcase to stay safe. She wrote about her life in a diary.


Itamar: A secret attic? That sounds really scary.


Safta Sara: Yes, it was. They had to sit quietly and they had to be careful. Sadly, the Nazis found them and took them away. But her diary survived, which helps us learn about her life and remember what happened. In her words, we see her hopes, fears, and daily life.


Itamar: I mean, I don't get it. Why did they have to hide?


Safta Sara: Because the Nazis were taking Jewish people to concentration camps. This went on in many places, such as Amsterdam, where Anne and my friend grew up.


Itamar: Oh man, that's so sad.


Safta Sara: It is. That's why we say 'Never Again.' We want to make sure such bad things don't happen to Jewish people — or anyone — ever again. We remember to stay strong and keep Israel safe.


Itamar: So, remembering helps us stay strong?


Safta Sara: Exactly, my dear. By remembering, we honor those who suffered and promise to protect each other, like a big family.
